## Summary

In this episode, Ross Simmonds interviews Matt Sims about why most organizations approach AI adoption incorrectly. Rather than treating AI as a technology implementation problem, Matt argues that companies should first redesign workflows, identify business constraints, and rethink how value is created before introducing AI.

The conversation explores AI transformation, workflow redesign, sales enablement, leadership, strategy, systems thinking, and organizational change. Throughout the interview, Ross guides the discussion toward practical advice for founders, executives, and marketing leaders looking to use AI as a competitive advantage rather than simply adopting new tools.

## Key Takeaways

### AI does not fix broken businesses

Organizations often assume AI will improve performance automatically.

Instead, AI accelerates existing systems.

If workflows are inefficient today, AI simply makes those inefficiencies happen faster.

---

### Start with workflows, not tools

Rather than asking:

> "Which AI tool should we buy?"

Companies should ask:

- Which workflow creates the biggest bottleneck?
- What business outcome are we trying to improve?
- How can AI redesign that workflow?

---

### Remove business constraints first

AI initiatives should begin by identifying the biggest constraint preventing business growth.

Examples include:

- Sales conversion
- Marketing efficiency
- Customer onboarding
- Delivery operations

Improving these constraints produces measurable business value.

---

### AI should increase speed of trust

One example discussed is using AI-generated meeting dossiers before customer conversations.

Instead of spending hours researching prospects manually, AI prepares detailed summaries that allow sales teams to build trust faster and reduce sales cycles.

---

### Strategy comes before automation

Organizations frequently automate existing processes without questioning whether those processes should exist.

The interview emphasizes redesigning business strategy before introducing AI into operations.

---

### CEOs should lead AI transformation

Matt argues that AI should not be delegated to a standalone "Head of AI."

Instead:

- CEOs should personally understand AI.
- Leadership teams should redesign workflows.
- AI adoption should become part of organizational strategy rather than isolated experimentation.

---

### AI changes execution—not thinking

While AI dramatically improves execution speed, strategic thinking remains the responsibility of leaders.

Competitive advantage increasingly comes from:

- Better strategy
- Better workflow design
- Better decision-making

rather than simply adopting more AI tools.

---

### The future belongs to organizations that adapt

Businesses that continuously redesign workflows and integrate AI into core operations will outperform those treating AI as another software purchase.

Transformation is ultimately about organizational learning rather than technology implementation.
