## Summary

In this interview, Richard van der Blom explains how professionals can build authority on LinkedIn by combining strong positioning, valuable content, strategic engagement, and proactive relationship building. He emphasizes that LinkedIn should be viewed as a business platform rather than a traditional social network and discusses how the platform's algorithm rewards meaningful interactions over vanity metrics.

The conversation covers LinkedIn thought leadership, algorithm signals, content creation, employee advocacy, conversion strategies, enterprise sales, and building sustainable content systems.

## Key Takeaways

### Authority starts before LinkedIn

Richard argues that LinkedIn doesn't create authority—it amplifies existing expertise. Professionals should first establish genuine expertise, then use LinkedIn to increase its visibility.

### Thought leadership requires three pillars

Successful LinkedIn thought leadership combines:

- Strong profile positioning
- Consistent, valuable content
- Strategic engagement

Neglecting any one of these weakens overall authority.

### Patience beats shortcuts

Building authority takes months or years rather than weeks. Richard warns against expecting rapid growth through templates or "LinkedIn hacks."

### Engagement is a strategy—not just commenting

Rather than randomly commenting throughout the feed, Richard recommends building systems that surface relevant conversations and engaging intentionally with target audiences.

### Saves are one of LinkedIn's strongest signals

Content that teaches something useful and gets bookmarked performs especially well.

Examples include:

- Infographics
- Short educational carousels
- Frameworks
- Practical checklists

### Personal profiles outperform Company Pages

Richard explains why executives and employees generate significantly more reach than Company Pages and recommends treating employee advocacy as the primary distribution engine.

### Conversion requires proactive outreach

Posting content alone rarely generates business.

Instead:

- Identify engaged prospects.
- Connect with them.
- Continue the conversation.
- Build relationships over time.

### Silent followers become customers

Many future clients never like or comment.

They consume content privately before eventually reaching out through email or LinkedIn.

### Consistency matters—but only with quality

Posting frequently without providing value doesn't help.

Richard recommends:

- Publishing two or more high-quality posts per week.
- Creating content around a clearly defined expertise.
- Building repeatable content systems.
