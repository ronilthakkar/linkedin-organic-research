## Full Post

Most companies are leaving 80% of their LinkedIn reach on the table. Not because they're inactive, but because they're following outdated rules.

They invest in the Company Page, hire someone to post three times a week, and then wonder why only 200 people see it.

Your Company Page and your personal profile aren't competitors.

You just need to treat them like a system, which most companies don't understand.

After analysing millions of posts for the Algorithm Insights Report 2026, this is what we see at the moment:

### 1. Personal profiles get 8× more reach and 12× the engagement of Company Pages.

Pages speak into the void.

People interact.

If your team isn't posting, your brand is invisible.

### 2. "Resharing with your own thoughts" is a no-go.

LinkedIn classifies this as low-effort copied content and suppresses it.

What works:

- Comment on the Company Page post.
- Use Instant Reshare.

Two actions.

Completely different outcome.

### 3. Your thought leaders are your highest-value distribution channel.

B2B buyers follow people, not logos.

Content from C-level profiles reaches a more relevant, more senior audience than brand content ever will.

### 4. C-level leaders creating original content receive up to 5× more views.

The algorithm elevates them as high-credibility sources.

A single post from your CEO can reach more qualified buyers in 48 hours than a month of Company Page posts.

### 5. Who engages matters as much as how many.

More than 80% engagement from internal colleagues is an algorithmic red flag.

External engagement from people outside your existing audience is the real accelerator.
