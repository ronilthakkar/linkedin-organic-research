## Full Post

(I know it's not good for engagement, but long post incoming...)

More and more prospects tell us the same thing on sales calls:

They have an agency. They've been working with them for a while. But they don't trust them anymore. (At least not in this new environment.)

That's a trust issue. And if I had to guess where it comes from, I'd put it in three buckets.

1. They don't care to adapt. Changing the playbook, learning, adjusting to a new reality. None of it interests them.

2. They don't know, and as a result, they don't have good answers. They might be trying, but when a client asks a difficult question, they ramble.

3. They know, but they have no conviction. The work is so new and so abstract that they can't communicate it clearly. They don't have a playbook they actually believe in.

Let me give you an example of the second one.

We're in talks with a billion-dollar company in the US. They told us we're competing against a US-based agency most people in this space know. The kind that portrays itself as the top name in AI search, locks you in for 12 months, and charges you $250K/year for… well, whatever it is that they claim to be doing.

When the prospect asked them a simple question about measurement, the CEO rambled.

If you've watched this person in webinars, you'd assume that if anyone has AI search figured out, it's them. Turns out even an agency at the supposed forefront is guessing!

To be fair, I don't think they don't care. I think they care a lot. It's just that this stuff is so new that having real conviction and communicating it clearly is hard.

We're not immune to that. In 2024 and 2025, we lost a lot of ground in AI search.

But when Zacharias and I got back into the trenches, we didn't just catch up. We started leading the way on how companies should approach this emerging channel.

We could do that because we had a strong foundation, and because we care. If we didn't, we wouldn't run courses, host workshops, develop frameworks, and build one tool after another.

Back to that deal: once we learned measurement was the gap that caught the other agency off guard, we addressed it head-on with our approach. That was reassuring to our prospect. That's all they needed up to that point.

Seeing all this makes me realize that there's huge potential in AI search, and Minuttia - AEO Agency is super well-positioned to capitalize on it.

Since the beginning, we'd been modest with our claims. Now we tell companies we help them "Dominate AI Search."

(In fact, that's the main headline on our homepage if you want to go and check.)

And we mean it. When we qualify someone and believe we can help them win, winning means dominating.
