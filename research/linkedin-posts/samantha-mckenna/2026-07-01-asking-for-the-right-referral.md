## Full Post

"Thanks for your email, I'm not the right person."

"Got it. Could you point me in the right direction of who is?"

😑😑😑😑😑😑😑😑😑😑😑😑😑😑😑

Those replies, if we get them at all, are about as standard as they come.

Training on how to do this right is in our **Referrals** deck, as part of the **#samsales Show Me You Know Me** masterclass series and one of the most impactful hours we teach.

Here's a better reply framework, straight out of our deck:

💡 Reply with gratitude

💡 Use EQ in your ask

💡 Offer logic

💡 Anticipate that they may want confidentiality

💡 Close with appreciation

**Example:**

> Hi Paige!
>
> Thanks for the note back and taking the time to reply.
>
> Wonder if you might know who a better contact is within the org?
>
> I realize it's my job to figure it out but, I'd be so appreciative of your direction.
>
> I'm of course happy to not mention your name, if you'd prefer I not, or mention it if you think it would help get a reply from your colleague.
>
> Thank you for considering and again for even taking the time to reply!
>
> Cheers,
> Sam

Why does this incredibly straightforward, obvious-as-all-get-out reply work more often than not?

Because the bar is so low in how buyers are often replied to that this reply is a welcome change, even if it should be the standard.

Be human.

Don't be lazy.

Always lead and close with gratitude.
