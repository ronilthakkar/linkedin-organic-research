## Full Post

Last week, I posted about soup and giving airline lounge access as a perk.

489 likes.

136,000+ impressions.

When we launch a new LinkedIn Executive Branding client, we divide our initial interviews into two segments:

1. Making them the thought leader of their category.
2. Learning everything we can about the day they were born until now.

Some love being open books, while others prefer to stay private.

But executive brands on LinkedIn create benefits across more than a dozen areas, with brand awareness being one of the biggest and talent attraction and retention among the most valuable.

Your network engages with your personal stories.

People want to:

- Learn from you.
- Be inspired by you.
- Get to know you.

Your current employees—and future top talent—also want to understand what it's like to work with you.

They want to know:

- Who you are.
- What experiences shaped you.
- What you believe about leadership and culture.
- Even something as simple as your favorite airline lounge soup.

Thought leadership is essential.

But your total addressable market (TAM) for expertise alone is limited.

Your personal stories are what build your brand, generate earned media opportunities, create transparency with your organization, and attract exceptional talent.

We're living in a trust economy.

Part of that trust comes from learning from experienced professionals.

The other part comes from understanding the human behind the expertise.

Whether that's sharing your thoughts on soup, travel perks, or a life-changing experience that others can learn from.
