## Full Post

Using LinkedIn Sales Navigator felt like cheating to me.

Across 15 years, I've created countless prospecting workflows, and here's one you can set up in about 10 minutes that's turned Sales Navigator into a lead machine for myself, my reps, and our clients.

### 1. Click **Lead Filters** on the homepage.

### 2. Under **Connections Of**

Add:

- Your executive team
- Senior cross-functional leaders
- Your manager
- Executive peers

You can add five people or thirty.

Just make sure you're connected with them first.

### 3. Add **Job Titles**

Target:

- Your ideal buyer
- Cross-functional stakeholders
- Decision-makers

### 4. Add **Current Company** or **Saved Accounts**

If your CRM integrates with Sales Navigator, import your territory.

Otherwise, manually add your top target accounts.

### 5. Set the **Geography**

Think broadly.

Even if you sell into the Northeast, introductions may come from someone sitting in California.

Use the widest relevant territory.

Review your results.

If nothing appears yet, save the search anyway and check it every week.

Sales Navigator automatically updates the list whenever:

- Your executives make new connections.
- Existing connections change companies.
- New buyers enter your target accounts.

Now you have a continuously refreshed source of warm introductions.

Instead of cold outbound, you can:

- Build stronger internal relationships.
- Show leadership you're proactive.
- Request warm introductions.
- Reach buyers faster.
- Build trust earlier.

This is one of more than a dozen prospecting plays included in our prospecting playbook.

You may never need traditional outbound again.
