## Full Post

I left a $550,000 job at 38 with no plan and very little runway.

By every measure that LinkedIn cares about, I was late.

Late to start something of my own.

Late to figure out what I actually wanted.

Late to build the thing that would eventually work.

Most people my age had a decade head start on their own business.

I had a huge burnout and a resignation letter.

Over the next seven years, I learned a lot about what's important and what isn't.

To start, the timeline you're panicking about is fake.

You can be lost at 22 and fine.

You can be broke at 28 and fine.

You can start over at 38 and be fine.

I was 38 with no employees, no investors, and no idea if any of it would work.

The age wasn't the problem.

The age was never the problem.

What mattered was getting a small number of things right and letting them compound.

Not faster.

Just right.

I spent the last year going through 250+ essays I've written, hours of voice notes, and 10 interviews with successful friends to find what those things actually were.

It became a 30-page field manual on how I built a $15M business without wrecking my life in the process.

Zero hustle tactics.

No growth hacks.

Just the 7 capacities that compounded over time.
