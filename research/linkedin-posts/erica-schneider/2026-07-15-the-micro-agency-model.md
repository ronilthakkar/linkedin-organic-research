## Full Post

There's a model between solo-and-grind until you burn out, and building a traditional agency you swore you never would.

We call this 3rd option the micro-agency model.

It's small by design, high-margin by intention, and built to last. It also guarantees you don't take home peanuts for years, working towards an exit that may never come.

Here are the 5 components of a micro agency:

### 1. The economic model

The foundation of the micro-agency is how you structure the money. The baseline is retainer-based, recurring revenue, with a finite client roster.

You have a handful of mid-to-high-tier retainer clients, with enough volume to de-risk the portfolio and enough recurring revenue to build on.

### 2. The engagement model

The engagement model is the four-phase journey every client moves through: audit, implement, optimize, scale.

You don’t reinvent each phase for each client or custom scope based on what comes up during the first call. You apply the same arc consistently, regardless of who the client is or what they do.

(You can swizzle around the micro deliverables and order of ops within each phase, but the phases are locked in tight.)

### 3. The marketing model

You market the problem you solve, the process you use to solve it, and the proof that it works.

MP3 baby.

You're not in the traffic business so you don’t need to generate hundreds of leads a year. You need a steady, reliable stream of the right people who already understand the problem, trust the process, and are ready to hire you.

### 4. The sales model

The framework is the sales cascade: direction setting, discovery, pitch, and close.

You lead the call with your point of view. You establish what you do, how you do it, and who it’s for before the prospect can reframe the conversation.

You're in charge of the call. You're not winging it.

### 5. The delivery model

The micro-agency delivery structure is called a pod and it has two parts:

The strategist and delivery team.

The strategist is often the founder. You run marketing, business development, and sales, and own all client relationships.

The second is the delivery team, horizontally rather than vertically integrated. Everyone on the delivery team touches all accounts. Flat rate per account. They earn more if they handle more accounts, which is a win-win for you and them.

The best part of the micro-agency model is that it scales to what makes sense for your life, given the season you're in.

A $50K/month business with one EA can be a micro agency. A $200K/month business with a three-person pod can be a micro agency.

The size isn't the point.

The margin is.

Both founders in the above scenarios are taking home a similar amount.
