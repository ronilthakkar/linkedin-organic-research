## Full Post

We advertise that we're for consultants doing $30k+ months, but Zoe was different.

She'd had a side hustle for years, and the last hurdle was quitting her full-time job so she could go all the f*ck in.

When she came to us 4 months ago, she was a few weeks away from doing just that.

So we got straight to work.

### 1. We helped her see the business she was actually running

She'd been ghostwriting for years but never called it a business. No standardized offer. No pricing she believed in.

Her expertise and systems were hiding in her head. So we defined the problem she solves, who she solves it for, and how she solves it.

Then we mapped that defined knowledge into SOWs, her website, her content, and sales calls.

### 2. We repositioned her offer

We anchored her whole business around one specific problem, the "accessible executive," and rebuilt her positioning, her offer, and her content to sell against it.

### 3. We standardized her chaotic delivery into one repeatable engagement

We built her engagement model with clear deliverables per phase and a premium floor she sticks to.

Now every new client walks through the same doors with the same expectations.

### 4. We rebuilt her sales process

She was great on discovery, but rough on closing.

So we ran her through our sales cascade framework. Discovery calls got longer (20 min → 45 min). Pricing conversations got shorter.

For the first time ever, she took control of sales conversations and stopped taking the bait.

### 5. We pivoted her content strategy

We shifted her content to roughly 2 problem, 2 process, 1 proof post per week (our MP3 strategy), written for VPs and founders at B2B tech companies.

Engagement dropped (intentionally), and inbound from buyers went up.

*(Your audience quality > your engagement rate. Every damn time.)*

Zoe's the same sharp, slightly chaotic writer she's always been.

We just built the container so the world could see it (and pay for it).

DM me if you're doing $30k+ months (or tenacious AF like Zoe), and ready to scale.
