## Full Post

Stop treating YouTube like a dumping ground. 🗑️

That's exactly what most B2B teams are doing right now.

They take last quarter's webinar recording, slap a title on it, upload it with the default thumbnail, and call it "video content." Same with podcast episodes (often just a static image + audio) and half-baked tutorial screen recordings.

Then they're shocked when:

- The video gets 200 views in 6 months.
- It never ranks in YouTube search.
- It never gets cited in LLMs.
- It never ranks in Google.
- It generates zero leads.

The best marketing teams treat YouTube with the same editorial mindset they treated their blog back in 2023. It's content with a purpose. It's video production with a goal. And it's storytelling rooted in best practices.

Is it easy?

Definitely not.

But in a time when YouTube videos are showing up in the SERP more than almost any other domain...

You can't ignore it.

The smartest brands in the world are already investing in YouTube.

We worked with a brand that had over 100 videos that were not being cited in LLMs or driving any traffic. We took those videos and applied best practices to them and they're on track to see an additional 100K views on that content.

What did we do?

1. Updated the thumbnails with best-in-class visuals.
2. Updated the titles so they were rooted in search intent.
3. Added descriptions that included gated assets.
4. Restructured the playlists to make logical sense.
5. Started producing new videos to keep the engine going.

Don't sleep on the power of YouTube in 2026.

Here's some data on YouTube that every B2B marketer needs to read before the end of the summer. It's filled with gold.
