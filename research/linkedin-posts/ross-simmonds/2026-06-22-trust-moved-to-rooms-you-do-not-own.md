## Full Post

By the time a buyer books a demo with you, the decision is mostly made. And it wasn't made on your website.

The B2B buying journey stopped being a funnel a while ago. It looks more like a web.

Before a prospect ever fills out a form, they have already:

- Read a few G2 reviews.
- Lurked a Reddit thread comparing you to two competitors.
- Watched a YouTube teardown of your SaaS.
- Asked ChatGPT and Claude to recommend the best options.

Most of that happens in places your brand does not control, and it shapes the shortlist whether the buyer notices or not.

The stages where someone decides who makes the cut have moved onto third-party turf:

- Reddit
- Review sites
- Independent communities

Those are also the same sources AI models pull from when generating answers.

Reddit is one of the most cited domains in AI Overviews and ChatGPT responses today.

Skip those conversations and the models have no reason to mention you when a buyer asks for recommendations.

Here's what our team at Foundation Marketing has done for billion-dollar brands across Martech, Payroll, HR, Cybersecurity, Sales, and more:

### Build authentic Reddit presences

Create transparent brand accounts and subreddits that answer questions and earn trust where category discussions already happen.

### Optimize existing YouTube libraries

Most brands already have valuable videos.

Updating titles, metadata, organization, and optimization helps those assets drive AI visibility and influence purchasing decisions.

### Use reviews to improve products

Negative reviews aren't only a reputation problem.

They're product feedback.

We use review analysis to improve both AI visibility and product roadmaps.

### Partner with niche YouTube creators

YouTube increasingly influences both traditional search and AI search.

Influencer partnerships help brands own high-intent searches and category conversations.

And so much more.

With the B2B buying journey looking this different...

Marketing teams need to think differently.

Remember:

None of this works if your own house isn't in order.

- Build a product worth recommending.
- Understand customer pain points.
- Monitor important prompts and keywords.
- Answer questions openly.
- Create content that's easy to cite.
- Give AI models a reason to reference your brand.

For CMOs:

1. Map where your category discussions actually happen.
2. Show up as a genuine expert.
3. Optimize both owned and off-site content for humans and AI.

The buying journey didn't become more complicated.

Trust simply moved to places you don't own.

The brands winning today are showing up there anyway.
