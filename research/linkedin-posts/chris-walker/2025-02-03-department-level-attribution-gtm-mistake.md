## Full Post

Even the Top 1% of Go-To-Market (GTM) teams I interact with make this simple, massively painful mistake 👇

Planning and measuring GTM based on which *department* sourced the lead.

Most SaaS companies are trying to implement a "Four Funnel" MQL Waterfall Model and it 100% will not fix their GTM.

"Marketing Sourced" vs. "SDR Sourced" vs. "Sales Sourced" vs. "Partner Sourced"

It sounds great and easy in theory, so they can divide lead source credit between Marketing, Sales, SDRs, and Partners. And then put together the most BASIC, INADEQUATE revenue model that looks great in an Excel spreadsheet, but will 100% fail in real life.

It's even being taught at the most popular "schools" for CMOs & CROs.

This model is considered a best practice in the industry and meanwhile is the main cause of Go-To-Market Dysfunction leading to Slowed Growth, GTM Team Misalignment, and Inflated CAC.

It's the equivalent of a Car Factory trying to decide which department made the car:

"30% of the cars got made by the people who put the wheels on"

"40% of the cars got made by the people who ordered the supplies and raw materials"

"10% of the cars got made by the people who do incoming inspection on the raw materials"

"20% of the cars got made by the people who assemble the dashboard electronics"

Do you see how stupid this is yet?

You need everyone in the Car Factory to do their part to build the car.

And you need everyone in your Revenue Factory to do their part to get every closed won customer and every renewal/expansion.

Trying to give credit to any department for building the car literally makes no sense.

And it's time to bring that common sense into B2B go-to-market.

Stop using department-level attribution as a replacement to your Strategic & Operational KPIs. Start planning, measuring and optimizing your GTM like a Unified Revenue Factory.

#finance #gtm #gotomarket #sales #marketing

I understand the "Four Funnel MQL Model" deeply, have implemented it many times, and personally recommended it from 2018–2022. But it will 100% fail in today's environment because it does not produce SCALABLE UNIT ECONOMICS.

This highly inefficient model makes it "easy" for Finance to do annual planning and try to measure ROI, yet basically impossible to scale, plan, forecast, diagnose, and optimize.

For mature companies, billions of dollars in enterprise value are on the line over the next few years... So be careful where you get your advice from.

All of the "best practices" and "frameworks" B2B companies get from Gartner, Forrester, and tech vendors were built for the Growth-at-All-Costs era and do not work in today's environment where sound unit economics are fundamentally required.
