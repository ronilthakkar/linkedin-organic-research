## Full Post

Who said B2B is boring?

One of the coolest things about Exit Five (to me) is the range of industries and types of companies in B2B that we see.

Yes, because I came up working at B2B SaaS MarTech/SalesTech companies, a bunch of folks from around that world are involved in Exit Five.

But over the last four years of the company, people started popping up saying, "Hey, B2B is so much more than marketing tech," and I said, "I KNOW!! We just needed to turn up the volume on that fact."

Today in our Exit Five Marketing Leaders community (VPs and CMOs in B2B companies over $50M in revenue), we have people working in hardware, software, manufacturing, cybersecurity, physical security, fitness, HR, finance, data, research, supply chain, distributors, and more. I am just riffing here sitting drinking my coffee on a Sunday morning, so I am missing some.

Plus many of them are not in big markets like San Francisco or New York City.

They are in Ohio, Wisconsin, Vermont.

We're not building for the LinkedIn-famous CMOs.

We're building for the 99% of B2B marketing leaders who don't have lots of LinkedIn followers, don't have an on-demand network from a Tier 1 VC firm, and aren't the same 10–20 names that have appeared on every "Best CMOs in B2B" list since 2015.

That is the whole value proposition of community.

We'll help make connections and build your network of peers.

In the last week alone I've seen myself, Matthew Carnevale, and Dan Murphy personally make introductions, connect members, and offer one-on-one help to leaders in our community in ways that simply wouldn't happen outside Exit Five.

I'm LONG on Exit Five.

Yes, I own the business and I'm the ultimate PR man, but I am LONG Exit Five—and thanks for pushing me to see the world beyond B2B MarTech ;)

OK, going to go enjoy this sunny Sunday in Vermont with my crew.
