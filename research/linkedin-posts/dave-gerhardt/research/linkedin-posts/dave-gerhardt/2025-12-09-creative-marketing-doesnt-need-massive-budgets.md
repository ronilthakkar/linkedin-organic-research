## Full Post

Creative marketing doesn't need massive budgets.

I think we use that as a crutch - oh yeah sure, I'd do that too if I had Ramp or Rippling's budget.

No. Creative marketing doesn't need massive budgets.

Some of my favorite campaign plays:

Instead of a $50k conference booth, we wrapped a nearby train station for 1/4 of the cost.

Result: Everyone thought we owned the event.

Instead of expensive testimonials and photoshoots, we built a wall of real social proof from Twitter and LinkedIn for the website.

Cost: $0

Result: Endless stream of authentic customer love

Instead of renting that big NASDAQ billboard in Times Square for a full day, we rented it for ~30 minutes back in 2018.

Cost: Fraction of normal price

Result: Social proof that lives on forever. I still have the pics on my phone.

Marketing is a game of attention.

You can find creative ways to get attention without breaking the whole budget to “do creative marketing.”

Execute differently than everyone else. Focus on memorable moments vs checking boxes.

That's the fun part of marketing to me. Give me the big budget, give me no budget. We're going to stand out and get attention either way.
