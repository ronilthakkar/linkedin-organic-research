# Research Methodology

This document outlines the methodology used to curate and organize this research repository.

---

# Research Goal

The objective of this project is to build a structured knowledge base of high-quality B2B SaaS practitioners who consistently publish practical insights on LinkedIn organic content strategy.

Rather than collecting large volumes of content, the focus was on identifying high-signal practitioners whose work could support future Product Marketing and Go-to-Market playbook development.

---

# Expert Selection Criteria

Experts were selected using the following criteria:

- Active practitioners rather than content aggregators
- Consistent publishing on LinkedIn
- Demonstrated expertise in B2B SaaS marketing
- Recognized thought leadership within their specialization
- Publicly available long-form content (where possible)

The selected experts collectively represent multiple areas of modern B2B marketing, including:

- Product Marketing
- Go-to-Market Strategy
- Founder-led Marketing
- Executive Branding
- Demand Generation
- AI Search
- Content Marketing
- LinkedIn Organic Growth
- Social Selling

---

# Research Collection Process

The research process consisted of five stages.

## 1. Expert Discovery

Identify practitioners who consistently publish practical, experience-driven marketing insights.

---

## 2. Content Review

Review recent LinkedIn posts and long-form video or podcast discussions to identify representative content.

---

## 3. Content Curation

Select content that best demonstrates each expert's strategic thinking, frameworks, and practical recommendations.

Rather than collecting every available post, representative examples were chosen to maximize research quality while minimizing redundancy.

---

## 4. Repository Organization

Organize the collected material into a consistent repository structure.

Each expert includes:

- Expert overview (README)
- Curated LinkedIn posts
- Long-form transcript or podcast notes (where available)
- Structured research notes

---

## 5. Knowledge Synthesis

Review collected content to identify recurring themes, messaging approaches, positioning strategies, and content patterns across multiple practitioners.

These observations form the basis for future playbook development.

---

# Repository Structure

The repository is organized into four primary components.

## LinkedIn Posts

Curated LinkedIn posts grouped by expert.

---

## YouTube Transcripts

Long-form interviews, podcasts, and video transcripts grouped by expert.

---

## Expert Profiles

Each expert includes a dedicated README describing:

- Background
- Why they were selected
- Research focus
- Key learning themes

---

## Research Documentation

Supporting documentation describing methodology, expert selection, and repository organization.

---

# Research Principles

Several principles guided the curation process.

## Quality over Quantity

Representative content was selected instead of attempting to archive every published post.

---

## Practitioner-first

Preference was given to marketers actively building products, companies, agencies, or communities rather than commentators.

---

## Diverse Perspectives

Experts were intentionally selected from complementary disciplines to provide broader coverage of modern B2B marketing.

---

## Reusable Knowledge

Research was organized to support future analysis, comparison, and playbook development.

---

# Technical Workflow

Repository organization and documentation were supported using modern AI-assisted development tools.

Additional automation utilities are included to simplify transcript collection and repository maintenance where appropriate.

---

# Limitations

This repository is not intended to be a complete archive of each expert's work.

Instead, it provides a curated selection of representative content suitable for structured research and future knowledge synthesis.

As the repository evolves, additional experts, transcripts, and supporting materials may be incorporated.

---

# Outcome

The completed repository serves as a structured research foundation for studying LinkedIn organic content strategy within B2B SaaS.

It combines curated practitioner content with consistent documentation and reusable organization, making it suitable for future competitive analysis, playbook creation, and Product Marketing research.
